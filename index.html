The discovery of Archimedes'
principle

Archimedes' principle, discovered by the ancient Greek mathematician Archimedes of Syracuse around 250 BCE, is a foundational concept in fluid mechanics. According to the principle, an object submerged in a fluid experiences an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of the fluid it displaces.

This insight came to Archimedes during a bath, where he observed that the water level rose as he entered, leading him to conclude that the displaced water's volume equaled the volume of his submerged body. This observation prompted him to articulate the principle, which provides a fundamental explanation for buoyancy.
