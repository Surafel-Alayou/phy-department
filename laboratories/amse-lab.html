Advanced Material and Solar Energy Lab (AMSE)

The Advanced Material and Solar Energy Lab (AMSE) was established in 2020 at Bahir Dar University. The lab focuses on research in emerging solar cell technologies and thin-film materials. It mainly supports condensed matter physics students in their studies and research. AMSE is organized and equipped to handle various projects and has been supported by funding from ISP, Cambridge Alborada, MIF, APS, and the Coimbra Group. The lab continues to grow and contribute to research in materials science and renewable energy.

Research areas

The Advanced Material and Solar Energy Lab (AMSE) focuses on research in emerging solar cells, material science, surface and interface properties, device performance, device stability, and thin-film technologies. Its work on next-generation solar cells, such as perovskite and organic photovoltaics, aims to enhance efficiency and scalability. The lab explores advanced materials for energy applications, studying their properties and interactions at surfaces and interfaces to improve device functionality. Research also addresses optimizing device performance and ensuring long-term stability under various conditions. Additionally, AMSE works on thin-film deposition techniques to develop high-quality materials for energy systems. Through these efforts, the lab contributes to innovations in renewable energy and sustainable technologies.

Dr. Amare Benor

Amare Benor received his PhD in Physics at Jacobs University Bremen, Germany, in 2008. He currently holds a position at Bahir Dar University in Ethiopia. Following on from his PhD study, he has worked on various surface engineering methods to tackle materials stability issues affecting the electronic structure of organic semiconducting and (opto-)electronic devices. In addition, he takes an interest in printing methods and electrodes patterning for applications in emerging optoelectronic devices. His current research, in Ethiopia, is supported by the International Science Program (ISP, IPPS ETH:03). During his stay with us at the Sirringhaus group he will continue his work on electrical stability of organic semiconductors, specifically, in connection to their impact on thermoelectronic energy harvesting devices.

Recent publications

  • Effect of orange fruit peel extract concentration on the synthesis of zinc oxide nanoparticles

    2024 | Analytical Science Advances

    Abstract

    In this investigation, the impact of reducing agent concentration on the synthesis of zinc oxide nanoparticles (ZnO NPs) was examined. During the synthesis, an assessment of ionic conductivity was carried out, revealing a significant increase in conductivity prior to the introduction of the reducing agent, followed by a sharp decrease upon its addition. Characterization of the ZnO NPs involved ultraviolet-visible spectroscopy, scanning electron microscopy, Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy, and, X-ray diffraction analysis. The outcomes suggest that the characteristics of the ZnO NPs are influenced by the concentration of the reducing agent during the synthesis process. Notably, the ZnO NPs synthesized with a higher concentration of reducing agent exhibited a narrower optical band gap and increased surface energy. Furthermore, employing a concentration of 0.5 v/v resulted in the rapid production of NPs with relatively uniform sizes. Conversely, concentrations below 0.5 v/v lead to slow formation, while concentrations exceeding 0.5 v/v yielded non-uniform NPs.



    DOI: https://doi.org/10.1002/ansa.202400023


  • Factors influencing surface coverage and structural organization of phosphonic acid self-assembled monolayers on zinc oxide

    2024 | Applied Physics A

    Abstract

    This study investigates the effects of solvent type, ODPA concentration, and solution temperature on the growth, surface coverage, and structural order of n-octadecylphosphonic acid (ODPA) self-assembled monolayers (SAMs) on zinc oxide (ZnO) surfaces. We explore the impacts of isopropanol and ethanol as solvents, considering their differing dielectric constants and lower toxicity compared to non-polar alternatives like toluene. The results indicate that ODPA SAMs formed more rapidly in isopropanol, with a growth rate of 0.7% per minute, compared to 0.2% per minute in ethanol. However, ethanol resulted in slightly higher surface coverage (97%) than isopropanol (96%). Moreover, increasing the ODPA concentration from 0.03 to 0.14 mM enhanced surface coverage; however, further increases in concentration did not lead to a corresponding increase in SAM coverage due to molecular interactions. The solution temperature also significantly influenced the growth rate of the SAM, with temperatures rising from 30 to 60 °C enhancing growth. However, higher temperatures (above 60 °C) affected the SAM structure, leading to defects in the monolayer. This study provides a comprehensive understanding of how solvent type, concentration, and solution temperature influence SAM formation, offering valuable insights for optimizing deposition conditions to enhance device performance in optoelectronic applications.



    DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s00339-024-08006-4


Regulations

  • Access Policy:
    • Only authorized personnel and students with approval are allowed to enter the lab.
    • Visitors must seek permission from the lab coordinator or supervisor before entering.
  • Safety Measures:
    • Lab users must wear appropriate safety gear, including gloves, goggles, and lab coats.
    • Report any accidents or hazards immediately to the lab supervisor.
    • Ensure proper handling and disposal of chemicals and hazardous materials.
  • Equipment Usage:
    • Operate lab equipment only after receiving proper training or guidance.
    • Do not remove or modify any equipment without prior approval.
    • Clean and return equipment to its designated place after use.
  • Data Management:
    • Document all experiments, results, and observations systematically in the provided lab records.
    • Backup digital data regularly to prevent loss of research data.
    • Maintain confidentiality of unpublished research findings.
  • Environmental Responsibility:
    • Conserve resources by minimizing waste and reusing materials where possible.
    • Switch off equipment, lights, and power outlets when not in use.
  • Code of Conduct:
    • Maintain a professional and respectful environment in the lab.
    • Follow all instructions provided by lab supervisors or coordinators.
    • Unauthorized experiments or activities are strictly prohibited.
