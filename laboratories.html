Laboratories

Our Physics Department is home to some of the most well-established laboratories within the College of Science. These state-of-the-art facilities play a crucial role in advancing research and education, particularly for our Master's and Ph.D. students. Our laboratories are equipped with cutting-edge technology, enabling students to carry out innovative research and successfully complete their theses and dissertations.

From experimental setups in fundamental physics to specialized equipment for advanced research, our labs provide the ideal environment for exploring a wide range of scientific phenomena. The hands-on experience gained in these labs prepares our students for successful careers in both academia and industry.

The department is committed to maintaining these high standards, continuously upgrading our facilities to support the latest research trends and scientific advancements. We take pride in fostering an environment where groundbreaking research is not just encouraged but facilitated, ensuring that our graduates are well-prepared to contribute to the global scientific community.

Laboratory precautions

1. Safety Gear: Always wear appropriate safety gear, including lab coats, safety goggles, and gloves. Use face shields or respiratory protection as needed for specific experiments.

2. Equipment Inspection: Inspect all laboratory equipment before use to ensure it's in good working condition. Report any damaged or malfunctioning equipment to lab supervisors

3. Chemical Handling: Follow proper procedures for handling, storing, and disposing of chemicals. Label all containers accurately, and never mix chemicals unless instructed to do so.

4. Electrical Safety: Ensure electrical equipment is grounded and in compliance with safety standards. Use circuit breakers and safety switches as required.

5. Emergency Procedures: Familiarize yourself with laboratory emergency protocols, including evacuation routes, fire extinguisher locations, and the use of safety showers and eyewash stations.

6. Personal Hygiene: Avoid eating, drinking, or applying cosmetics in the laboratory. Wash your hands thoroughly after handling chemicals, specimens, or equipment.

7. Experimental Procedures: Read and understand experimental procedures and protocols before starting any experiment. Follow instructions precisely and consult with a supervisor or instructor when in doubt.
