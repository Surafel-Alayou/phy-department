Background

Physics department has had an early foundation and a strong tradition of conducting research seminars in BDU. As evident in the picture beneath taken in the 1970s, teachers from different departments are seen attending seminar explanations given by Mr. Wolf probably about the oscilloscope's operation he had stood holding. Though most of the instructors in the picture were from other fields of studies than physics, their intellectual life style of appreciating such physics seminars is astonishing.

Standing from camera left to right in the picture are: Nigussie Kassahun (Pedagogy), Hailyesus Teklu (Physics), Mulugeta Tesfaye (Geography), Ayalew Gebrie (Psychology), Awol Endris (English),Tekie Assihun (Physics), Gebre-Egizabher Kahsay (PhD, Physics), Ahmed Mohammed (Mathematics) and Wolf (Physics).

Vision and mission

Vision

Our vision is to become a leading center for physics research and education, known for our dedication to advancing knowledge. We aim to inspire creativity, deepen understanding of the universe's basic principles, and lead in innovative research.

Mission

Our mission is to provide an exceptional and inclusive educational experience, equipping students with the skills and knowledge to excel in physics and beyond. We are dedicated to conducting transformative research that advances science, technology, and society. By fostering a collaborative environment and nurturing intellectual curiosity, we aim to prepare the next generation of physicists and contribute to solving some of the world's most pressing challenges through the insights and applications of physics.
